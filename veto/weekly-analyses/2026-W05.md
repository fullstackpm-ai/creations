## Week 4 → Week 5: Pattern Evolution

### Context: What Week 5 Actually Was

Week 4 revealed behavioral patterns. Week 5 was **acknowledging and internalizing** those patterns - not immediately performing better.

**The metrics declined because:**
- Processing new self-awareness takes cognitive energy
- You can't simultaneously have major realizations AND perform as if you've already integrated them
- Behavior change doesn't happen at the turn of a knob

**This is not failure. This is the natural human path:**
1. Discovery (W04) → 2. Acknowledgment (W05) → 3. Internalization (ongoing) → 4. Gradual behavior shift (future)

### Veto's Purpose (Calibrated)

| Aspect | Incorrect Assumption | Correct Understanding |
|--------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **End goal** | Just a mirror for self-discovery | Drive behavior change |
| **Design flaw** | — | Expected behavior adoption from day 1 |
| **Reality** | — | Humans are complex; path to behavior change is longer than 2 weeks |
| **Week 5** | "Operator mode failed" | Part of the journey toward behavior change |

**Veto's purpose remains:** A system that drives behavior.

**The learning:** The path from "system exists" to "system drives behavior" runs through discovery → acknowledgment → internalization → gradual adoption. Expecting to skip those phases was a design flaw in short-term expectations, not a flaw in the system's purpose.

## Questions for Next Week

1. **Sleep threshold:** Is 5.5h the breaking point? This week, every 5.5h sleep day had lower performance. Test: prioritize 7+ hours and see if weekday cliff flattens.

2. **Meeting load management:** Can you protect Monday/Wednesday mornings from meetings? The data suggests meeting-heavy days destroy deep work capacity for that day AND the next.

3. **Structured task framing:** Will explicitly naming the ONE task before each shallow block prevent drift? Track focus scores for named vs unnamed blocks.

4. **Internalization progress:** Which W04/W05 insights feel closer to "identity-level" now? Which still feel like "information I know but don't act on"?

5. **Authority hesitation:** What specifically triggers the hesitation? Is it fear of conflict, imposter syndrome, or something else? The next time you hesitate to exercise authority, capture what you're feeling in the moment.
